Predictive Fall Prevention

A comprehensive analysis of Haven's technology, market opportunity, and the science behind predictive fall prevention.

Executive Summary

Falls are the leading cause of injury-related death among adults aged 65 and older, claiming over 38,000 lives annually and generating $80+ billion in direct healthcare costs (CDC, 2024). Despite decades of investment in fall detection technology, every commercial solution on the market today shares a fundamental flaw: they react to falls after they happen.

Haven Home Wellness is building the first predictive fall prevention platform — a passive, privacy-preserving sensor ecosystem that detects subtle gait deterioration weeks before a fall occurs, enabling proactive clinical intervention instead of emergency response.

The Problem: Why Current Solutions Fail

According to the CDC's WISQARS, one in four adults aged 65+ falls each year in the United States. Of those 14 million annual falls, approximately 3 million result in emergency department visits, 800,000 lead to hospitalizations, and more than 38,000 result in death.

The economic burden is staggering. The National Council on Aging estimates that fall-related medical costs exceed $80 billion annually, with Medicare shouldering approximately 75% of that cost. By 2030, these numbers are projected to nearly double.

Yet every commercially available solution — Life Alert, Medical Guardian, Apple Watch fall detection — operates on the same reactive model: wait for a fall to occur, detect the impact, and then alert someone. This approach addresses response time but does nothing to prevent the fall itself.

Research consistently shows that fall risk does not appear suddenly. A 2023 study published in Gait & Posture demonstrated that measurable gait deterioration can be observed 2 to 6 weeks before a fall event. The data is there. The gap is that no consumer platform collects, analyzes, or acts on it continuously.

The Technology: Five-Platform Ecosystem

Haven's architecture is built on the principle that the most powerful health data is the data collected passively, continuously, and without requiring any action from the person being monitored.

Steadfast™ — Passive Plug-in Sensors

Small plug-in devices placed in the bedroom, bathroom, and kitchen — the three rooms where 78% of in-home falls occur. They detect movement patterns, room-to-room transitions, and presence without cameras, microphones, or any wearable component. Zero compliance required.

Stride™ — Wearable Intelligence Overlay

Stride integrates with the Apple Watch, Fitbit, or Garmin the senior already owns. It continuously monitors gait cadence, step symmetry, walking speed, activity levels, and sleep patterns to build a personalized movement baseline. Deviations trigger escalating risk indicators.

Vigil™ — Optional Computer Vision

For higher-risk individuals or institutional settings, Vigil adds pose estimation and gait analysis via computer vision. It delivers a three-tier risk score — Low, Watch, or Elevated — in real time. Entirely optional, privacy-tiered, and family-controlled.

Anchor™ — Edge Computing Hub

All sensor, wearable, and vision data flows to Anchor — a local edge computing hub inside the home. Anchor processes everything on-device. Raw data never leaves the home. Only derived insights are sent outbound to the family dashboard. This architecture satisfies HIPAA data handling principles by design.

Beacon™ — Family Dashboard

Available via the Haven app or any web browser, Beacon displays real-time risk scores, trend graphs, tiered alerts with AI-generated context, achievement milestones, and weekly health narratives written in human language — not clinical charts. Designed for the adult child, not the clinician.

The Science: Why Gait Predicts Falls

A 2024 study published in Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence demonstrated that machine learning models analyzing temporal gait features achieved 93.6% classification accuracy for fall risk in elderly populations.

Key gait parameters that correlate with elevated fall risk include reduced walking speed (below 0.8 m/s), shortened stride length, increased double-support time, greater step-to-step variability, and asymmetric gait patterns. These changes are often imperceptible to family members but are measurable by sensor systems operating continuously.

Research from the NIH National Institute on Aging further establishes that gait deterioration correlates with cognitive decline, cardiovascular events, and hospitalization risk — making continuous gait monitoring a potentially foundational health metric for aging populations.

Market Opportunity

The global elderly monitoring market is projected to reach $22+ billion by 2033, driven by three forces: the aging of the baby boomer generation (10,000 Americans turn 65 every day), the growing preference for aging in place (90% of seniors prefer to remain in their homes), and the escalating healthcare costs associated with falls.

Haven occupies a unique competitive position as the only platform combining predictive analytics with passive, privacy-preserving sensing and existing wearable integration.

The primary buyer is not the senior — it is the adult child, typically a 45-to-65-year-old professional managing care from a distance. Haven's product design, pricing, and marketing are built entirely around this buyer persona.

Regulatory Pathway & Reimbursement

The paper details Haven's FDA 510(k) regulatory pathway for Vigil as a Class II medical device, using BioSensics LEGSys as the predicate device. The 510(k) clearance is additive upside — not a gating dependency for the consumer launch.

Additionally, the paper covers the Medicare Remote Physiologic Monitoring (RPM) reimbursement opportunity under CPT codes 99454, 99457, and 99458 — a potentially significant recurring revenue stream.

Frequently Asked Questions

What makes Haven different from Life Alert or Apple Watch fall detection?

Life Alert and Apple Watch detect falls after they happen — they are reactive. Haven is predictive. It monitors subtle gait changes to identify increasing fall risk weeks before a fall occurs, requiring zero compliance from the senior.

Does Haven use cameras?

Haven's core system uses no cameras. Vigil is an optional computer vision module for higher-risk situations, entirely family-controlled and privacy-tiered. All data processing happens locally inside the home.

How accurate is gait-based fall prediction?

Peer-reviewed research demonstrates 90–95% sensitivity. A 2024 study in Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence reported 93.6% accuracy using temporal gait features from a single walking cycle.

Who is Haven designed for?

Haven is designed for adult children caring for aging parents who want to live independently. The primary buyer and daily dashboard user is the family caregiver — typically a 45-to-65-year-old professional managing care from a distance.
