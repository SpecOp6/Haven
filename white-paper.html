Predictive Fall Prevention White Paper

A comprehensive analysis of Haven's technology, market opportunity, and the science behind predictive fall prevention.

Executive Summary

Falls are the leading cause of injury-related death among adults aged 65 and older, claiming over 38,000 lives annually and generating $80+ billion in direct healthcare costs. Despite decades of investment in fall detection technology, every commercial solution on the market today shares a fundamental flaw: they react to falls after they happen.

Haven Home Wellness is building the first predictive fall prevention platform — a passive, privacy-preserving sensor ecosystem that detects subtle gait deterioration weeks before a fall occurs, enabling proactive clinical intervention instead of emergency response.

The Technology

Haven's platform combines passive plug-in sensors, wearable intelligence overlaying Apple Watch, Fitbit, and Garmin, and optional computer vision — all processed locally through an edge computing hub. No raw data ever leaves the home.

The white paper details the complete technical architecture across all five platforms: Steadfast (sensors), Stride (wearable gait intelligence), Vigil (computer vision), Anchor (edge hub), and Beacon (family dashboard).

Market Opportunity

The global elderly monitoring market is projected to reach $22+ billion by 2033. Haven occupies a unique position — the only platform combining predictive analytics with passive, privacy-preserving sensing and wearable intelligence integration.

Regulatory & Reimbursement

The white paper covers Haven's FDA 510(k) regulatory pathway for Class II medical devices and the Medicare CPT 99454 reimbursement opportunity for remote physiologic monitoring — a critical revenue enabler that validates the clinical utility of continuous movement data.

Financial Model

Detailed five-year financial projections across three revenue models: direct-to-consumer (DTC), B2B partnerships with senior living operators, and OEM/ISP channel integration. The paper includes unit economics, customer acquisition cost analysis, and path to profitability.
