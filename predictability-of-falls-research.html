The Scale of the Crisis

Your parent won't tell you about the near misses.

More than one in four adults over 65 falls every year. What's more unsettling: less than half of them tell their doctor. They don't want to worry their families. They don't want to lose their independence. So the early warning signs — the slower walk, the 3am trips to the bathroom, the subtle lean — go unnoticed until something serious happens.

And when something serious does happen, the consequences cascade. A hip fracture means surgery. Surgery means hospitalization. Hospitalization leads to immobility, muscle loss, and cognitive decline. Research shows that 25% of seniors who fracture a hip die within 12 months — not from the fall itself, but from everything the fall sets in motion.

Key Finding
"Falling once doubles your chances of falling again. One out of 10 falls results in an injury that restricts daily activity or requires medical attention." — CDC, January 2026

The fall death rate in the United States has climbed 21% over the past six years — from 64.7 per 100,000 older adults in 2018 to 78.4 per 100,000 in 2024. This isn't an aging problem that's being solved. It's an aging crisis that's accelerating. But it doesn't have to be.

The Science of Prediction

Gait changes before the fall. That's the window.

Here is what decades of biomechanical research have established with remarkable consistency: a person's walking pattern — their gait — begins changing long before they fall. The changes are subtle. Imperceptible to the naked eye. But measurable. And predictive.

A 2024 study published in Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence found that AI models using gait features extracted from computer vision achieved 93.6% accuracy in fall risk classification — using as little as a single gait cycle of data. The critical finding: increased cadence and shortened step times were strongly associated with elevated fall risk, changes families and even clinicians typically can't detect without specialized measurement.

Gait Signal What It Means Risk Level
Decreased gait velocity Slower walking speed; reduced confidence in stability Elevated
Shortened stride length Conservative step pattern; compensation for balance issues Elevated
Increased cadence (steps/min) Faster, choppier steps — a key predictor found in 2024 research Elevated
Increased gait variability Inconsistency between steps; reduced motor control Watch
Stance-phase asymmetry Unequal weight distribution; possible musculoskeletal issue Watch
Night-time bathroom frequency Increased nocturnal activity correlates with fall risk elevation Watch
Stable, consistent gait pattern Baseline normal movement — no significant deviation Low Risk

The challenge has always been continuous measurement. Clinical assessments — like the Timed Up and Go test — are accurate but require a clinical setting, scheduled appointments, and active participation. They capture a single snapshot, not the longitudinal pattern. And seniors who might most benefit from monitoring are least likely to attend regular assessments.

Emerging Research

What the evidence now confirms.

The last five years have produced an extraordinary convergence of research validating what Haven Home Wellness was built to deliver: passive, continuous, AI-powered monitoring that learns what's normal for each individual and detects when that changes.

Gait · AI Prediction
AI Gait Models Achieve 93.6% Accuracy in Fall Risk Classification
A 2024 study applying computer vision and gradient-boosting machine learning to gait data found that temporal gait features — particularly cadence and step timing — predicted fall risk with exceptional accuracy from a single gait cycle.
Lim et al. Frontiers in Artificial Intelligence, Vol. 7, August 2024
Passive Monitoring · Feasibility
82% of Participants Trusted Passive Sensors to Predict Fall Risk
The Sense4Safety study at University of Pennsylvania deployed passive in-home depth sensors with machine learning to identify escalating fall risk in real time. 82% of participants expressed confidence in the system's ability to predict falls and generate actionable alerts.
Demiris et al. Journal of Gerontology, May 2025
Wearables · Gait Analysis
Wearable IMU Sensors Achieve 81.6% Fall Risk Accuracy Over 6 Months
A prospective study of 171 community-dwelling older adults used chest-worn inertial measurement units during a 10-meter walk. Gait variability, complexity, and smoothness predicted falls over the following 6-month period with 86.7% sensitivity.
Scientific Reports, Nature Portfolio, 2021
ICT Interventions · Systematic Review
Technology-Based Interventions Significantly Reduce Fall Incidence
A systematic review and meta-analysis of ICT-based fall prevention interventions found meaningful reductions in fall frequency among older adults — validating technology as a credible primary intervention modality, not just an adjunct.
Lee, Yi, Lee. Gerontology & Geriatrics, SAGE Publications, 2024

It's never just a fall.

A fall doesn't end when the ambulance leaves. It triggers a cascade — physical, emotional, and financial — that reshapes families for years. The average fall-related hospitalization costs over $30,000. Medicare covers some of it. Families absorb the rest.

The Haven Approach

What changes when the home pays attention.

Haven Home Wellness was built on a single conviction: the technology to prevent the majority of serious falls already exists. What has been missing is a system that works without requiring anything of the senior — no buttons to press, no wearables to remember, no cameras to accept. Just a home that quietly notices what matters.

Haven Platform Architecture
Five integrated platforms of passive intelligence — building a complete picture of fall risk over time.
Steadfast™ — Passive Plug-In Sensors
Plug-in sensors detect movement, presence, and transitions throughout the home — bedroom, bathroom, kitchen. No cameras. Always on. Zero compliance required. The ambient foundation layer that never misses a signal.
Stride™ — Gait Intelligence
Integrates with Apple Watch, Fitbit, and Garmin to passively track step frequency, stride length, sleep quality, and balance trends over time — building a longitudinal baseline for each individual.
Vigil™ — Computer Vision Analysis
For highest-risk seniors, edge-based computer vision provides real-time gait and posture analysis with full privacy control. Privacy-tiered and family-controlled. The most data-rich signal layer in the stack.
Anchor™ — Edge Computing Hub
In-home edge processor that fuses all sensor and wearable data locally — raw data never leaves the home. Derived insights are delivered to Beacon. Matter-compatible with Apple Home, Google Home, Alexa, and SmartThings.
Beacon™ — Family Dashboard
The convergence layer — a daily risk score synthesized from every signal source, delivered to families with context, trends, and tiered alerts. Not binary alarm. Intelligent, compassionate intelligence.

The science is settled: gait deterioration precedes falls. Behavioral changes precede falls. Nighttime movement patterns change before falls. Haven is the first consumer platform designed to detect all of these signals, passively, continuously, and without placing any burden on the people being protected.
