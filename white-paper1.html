White Paper
A comprehensive analysis of Haven's technology, market opportunity, and the science behind predictive fall prevention.

Executive Summary

Falls are the leading cause of injury-related death among adults aged 65 and older, claiming over 38,000 lives annually and generating $80+ billion in direct healthcare costs. Despite decades of investment in fall detection technology, every commercial solution on the market today shares a fundamental flaw: they react to falls after they happen.

Haven Home Wellness is building the first predictive fall prevention platform — a passive, privacy-preserving sensor ecosystem that detects subtle gait deterioration weeks before a fall occurs, enabling proactive clinical intervention instead of emergency response.

The Technology

Haven's platform uses multi-modal radio frequency sensing — combining Wi-Fi Channel State Information (IEEE 802.11bf) with millimeter-wave FMCW radar — to create a continuous, passive picture of an individual's movement patterns within their home. No cameras, no microphones, no wearables.

The white paper details the complete technical architecture, from sensor placement strategy through edge AI processing, longitudinal gait profiling, anomaly detection algorithms, and clinical data delivery.

Market Opportunity

The global elderly monitoring market is projected to reach $22+ billion by 2033. Within this space, the fall detection radar market alone is growing at 37% CAGR, and the Wi-Fi sensing market at 23.8% CAGR. Haven occupies a unique position at the intersection of these growth vectors — the only platform combining predictive analytics with passive, privacy-preserving sensing.

Regulatory & Reimbursement

The white paper covers Haven's FDA 510(k) regulatory pathway for Class II medical devices and the Medicare CPT 99454 reimbursement opportunity for remote physiologic monitoring — a critical revenue enabler that validates the clinical utility of continuous gait data.

Financial Model

Detailed five-year financial projections across three revenue models: direct-to-consumer (DTC), B2B partnerships with senior living operators, and OEM/ISP channel integration. The paper includes unit economics, customer acquisition cost analysis, and path to profitability.
