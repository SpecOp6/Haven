What a single fall sets in motion

It's never just a fall.

A fall doesn't end when the ambulance leaves. It triggers a cascade — physical, emotional, and financial — that reshapes entire families.

The Physical Cascade
A hip fracture means surgery. Surgery means hospitalization. Hospitalization means immobility. 25% of seniors who fracture a hip die within 12 months — not from the fall, but from everything it set in motion.
The Loss of Home
Most seniors who experience a serious fall never return to full mobility. Many leave the home they raised their family in — not because they wanted to, but because the fall made the decision for them.
The Family Impact
Average fall hospitalization: over $30,000. But the real cost is the guilt of not knowing sooner. The what-ifs that follow you. The fear every time the phone rings from an unknown number.
"By the time a fall happens, the window to prevent it has already closed."

How It Works

Invisible protection.
Visible peace of mind.

Haven learns what "normal" looks like, then watches for the subtle changes that precede a fall — days or weeks before it happens.

1
Set Up in Minutes
Plug in small radar sensors around the home. Connect a wearable they already own. Complete a quick baseline assessment. No technician, no complexity.
2
Learn Their Normal
Over 14 days, Haven builds a personal behavioral baseline — gait patterns, sleep rhythms, movement habits. Not population averages — what's normal for them.
3
Predict the Drift
The AI detects subtle deviations — stride shortening, nighttime trips increasing, balance declining. Individually easy to miss. Together they tell the whole story.
4
Protect Before the Fall
Beacon alerts your family with context — not a panic alarm. "Mom's stride shortened 12% this week. Here's what that could mean and what you can do."

About Haven

Bridging the gap between
aging adults and AI.

Haven Home Wellness was founded on a simple observation: artificial intelligence is transforming every industry on earth — except the one that matters most to the people who are most vulnerable. Older adults are the fastest-growing demographic in the country, and the technology designed to protect them hasn't meaningfully evolved in decades.

We're changing that. Haven uses ambient sensors, wearable intelligence, and predictive AI to detect the subtle behavioral changes that precede a fall — days or weeks before it happens. No cameras in the bedroom. No clinical interfaces. No panic buttons. Just quiet, continuous intelligence working in the background so families can stop worrying and start knowing.

We're a small team of two co-founders who believe technology should be invisible, intelligence should be proactive, and the home should always feel safe. We built Haven because someone had to — and because the cost of waiting is measured in lives, not dollars.
